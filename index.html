OLYMPIC GOLD MEDALIST

After first beating Belgian boxer Yvon Becaus at the Rome Olympics in 1960, Ali went on to fight and win against Gennady Shatkov and Tony Madigan. He made the final against Poland's Zbigniew Pietrzykowski, much stronger and more experienced than Ali, in which the judges decisions were unanimous. Ali won the Olympic gold medal.

Ali was against the war in Vietnam and refused to be inducted into the armed forces, saying "I ain't got no quarrel with those Vietcong." As a result, Ali was convicted of draft evasion and sentenced to five years in prison, fined $10,000 and banned from boxing for three years.

Muhammad Ali received the Liberty Medal for his long-time role as an advocate for humanitarian causes, civil rights and religious freedom. Ali was recognized as the "Champion of Freedom" for his unrelenting, persistent fight for equality, peace, and justice in every area of life all over the world.

EQUAL RIGHTS ADVOCATE

37 CAREER KNOCKOUTS

Muhammad Ali was widely regarded by boxing commentators, historians and fans worldwide as one of the greatest professional boxers of all time. Ali boasts a total of 37 knockouts throughout all 61 fights of his boxing career.

Originally born as Cassius Marcellus Clay Jr., he later changed his name to Muhammad Ali after joining the Nation of Islam, stating "Cassius Clay is a slave name." In 1964, he joined the Nation of Islam citing religious beliefs.
